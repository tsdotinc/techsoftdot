VibavaLakshmi Pooja Mahimai


The Vaibhav Laxmi Vrat is a fast dedicated to Goddess Laxmi. It is done to get relief from financial miseries, debt, obstacles in life and poverty, and to increase the flow of wealth and fulfill desires. The fast is kept for 11 or 21 Fridays. It can be observed by both men and women. Unlike other Vrats dedicated to Goddess Laxmi, this fast is not bound to any month or season. It can be observed anytime during the year and it does not require to follow very strict parameters of Vrat.

Importance of Vaibhav Laxmi Vrat. The Vrat procedure was shared by Goddess Laxmi Herself to her devotee Sheela. She had lost all her wealth due to her husband resorting to gambling and other addictions. Goddess Laxmi visited Sheela in the disguise of an old woman and explained to her the importance and procedure of the Vrat. Sheela observed the Vrat methodically for 21 Fridays and Her husband eventually transformed and started working hard. Sheela regained all the last wealth and in-flow of wealth increased in her house. Inspired by this, other women in the neighborhood too started observing this Vrat.

Before beginning the Vrat a Sankalp or commitment of observing the fast for 11 or 21 days is made. On the day of Vrat, a full day fast is observed by the devotee. Puja altar is decorated, a Kalash is installed at puja altar and a food offering along with a silver or gold item is kept. The Vrat Katha is read, Laxmi mantra is chanted, and puja is performed. On the conclusion day, a special prasad is prepared and 11 married women and invited and gifts, Vrat book, and prasad are offered to them.

According to Indian Hindu scriptures, worship of Goddess Lakshmi is done for the achievement of happiness, prosperity, wealth, glory and happiness. It is believed that worshiping Goddess Lakshmi with the entire legislative law attains wealth, splendor and happiness. In Hinduism, various forms of Goddess Lakshmi are worshiped in which their Dhana Lakshmi means Vaibhav Laxmi Swaroop is very dear to the people. Therefore, worship of Shri Yantra is also done in Vaibhav Laxmi fast. According to Hindu beliefs, every day of the week lies in the worship of some goddess. Observing the Vrat with devotion and faith is believed to fulfill any desire including growth in business, finding a suitable match for marriage, progeny, knowledge, success in studies, growth in career, and wealth.
